Conceptual Note

  • In recent years, there has been a growing emphasis on improving the quality of higher education institutions (HEIs) in many countries around the world. This is driven by the recognition that HEIs are crucial in producing skilled and knowledgeable graduates, advancing research and innovation, and promoting economic growth and social development.

  • To institutionalise a quality culture in HEIs, it is important to focus on four key plenary topics. First, enhancing research quality in HEIs requires a focus on assessment and improvement strategies that promote the highest standards of research excellence. This includes investing in research infrastructure, supporting interdisciplinary collaborations, and ensuring that research outputs are rigorous, relevant, and impactful.

  • Second, enhancing higher education quality through information communication & technology (ICT) is essential for ensuring students receive a world-class education that prepares them for the digital age. This includes leveraging ICT to enhance teaching and learning, promoting digital literacy among students and faculty, and leveraging data analytics to support evidence-based decision-making.

  • Third, strategic approaches of HEIs towards achieving Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are crucial for ensuring that HEIs play a proactive role in addressing the major global challenges of our time, such as climate change, poverty, and inequality. This includes integrating sustainability into the curriculum, promoting community engagement and partnerships, and measuring and reporting on the impact of HEIs on the SDGs.

  • Finally, the Higher Education Institution's strategies for implementing the National Education Policy (NEP) are critical for ensuring that HEIs align with the national education agenda and contribute to national development goals. This includes promoting academic freedom and autonomy, ensuring that HEIs are inclusive and equitable, and leveraging innovative approaches to teaching and learning that foster creativity and critical thinking.

  • Overall, institutionalising a quality culture in HEIs requires a multifaceted approach that focuses on research quality, ICT, SDGs, and NEP. By addressing these key plenary topics, HEIs can position themselves as leaders in the global knowledge economy and contribute to a more sustainable and equitable future for all.

Objectives

In the move toward enhancing quality in higher education in general and universities in specific, the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) has introduced a new assessment and accreditation process since July 2017. Considering the character of its process that encompasses both qualitative and quantitative dimensions and represents an explicit Paradigm Shift making it ICT enabled, objective, transparent, scalable and robust. There is an urgent need to build competencies of IQAC members at the institutional level and to develop an understanding of the nuances involved in the current process of assessment and accreditation.

It is essential to create a deeper understanding of quality related to data mining, data capturing across the NAAC framework and its specific criteria so that the institutions develop new benchmarks and best practices. The capacities of key personnel driving quality initiatives in their respective institutions are to be developed and nurtured. Hence the seminar on NAAC Assessment and Accreditation- Building Institutional Competencies will focus on issues related to how institutions can develop their self-improvement initiatives to promote innovation and strengthen quality in their respective institutions.

Outcome

  • Through the seminar the competencies of key personnel in universities/institutions with regard to quality awareness will be identified and efforts will be made to disseminate and transfer quality ideas to all concerned in their respective institutions. As a key outcome institutions will gain increased awareness on the overall approach to the new NAAC quality Framework.

  • Further IQAC faculty members and administrators in their respective institutions will acquire capabilities and conceptual clarity of the revised NAAC Assessment and Accreditation (A& A) framework.

  • The key university personnel develop institutional capacity building in understanding Quality Indicator Framework (QIF).

  • Institutional personnel develop a deeper understanding in the process of developing their capacity and competencies for preparation of SSR, data mining and articulating as per NAAC framework.

  • Institutions develop the efficiency and developmental procedures in effective management of IQAC in their institutions.

  • Institutions develop several support systems and mentoring departments in quality assurance process.

  • Enable Participating institutions to evolve best practices on their own and for enhancing changes of their Institutions and quality in higher education in general.

  • Institutions develop quality as part of institution culture and promote continuous quality improvement processes at institutional levels.

#_ABOUT_US_

The University

CHRIST (Deemed to be University), began as Christ College in 1969 offering undergraduate programmes in Sciences, Humanities, Social Sciences and Commerce. From 1992 it began offering Masters programmes in Social Sciences and Sciences, and Management. It was the first Institution in Karnataka and one of first 14 in India to be accredited by NAAC in 1997. It also became the first college in Karnataka to be granted Autonomous status by UGC in 2004. It was the first Institution in South India to be reaccredited with NAAC A+ Grade in 2005. In 2006 it was accorded the College with Potential for Excellence status by UGC. Based on the quality of academics at the Institution, past performance, and future potential, the Ministry of Human Resources Development, Government of India conferred the status of Deemed to be University on the Institution vide Notification No. F. 9-34/2007-U.3(A) dated 22 July 2008.

Primarily a liberal arts Institution, CHRIST (Deemed to be University) offers 51 Bachelor, 50 Master, 19 MPhil and 20 Doctoral programmes in Humanities, Social Sciences, Sciences, Commerce, Management, Law, Engineering, Architecture and Education to about 22500 students from all the states and union territories of India and 58 other countries.

Apart from being ranked well since 2007 in almost all higher education ranking surveys , the University has also been ranked in QS Asia, QS India and QS BRICS Ranking. CHRIST (Deemed to be University) is also ranked 301+ in the Times Higher Education University Impact Ranking 2019. In the raking it is grouped in 101–200th for Quality Education and 201–300th for Gender Equity, globally.

With ‘Excellence and Service’ as the Vision, the University is actively involved in community development through student involvement. The University has slum and rural development projects with a focus on women and children in the city of Bangalore, in the states of Karnataka, Kerala, Maharashtra and Chhattisgarh directly benefiting over 10,000 families. The students and staff of the University sponsor the primary education of over 900 students in the slums of Bangalore annually through voluntary contribution

National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC)

The National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) is an organisation that assesses and accredits higher education Institutions (HEIs) in India. It is an autonomous body funded by University Grants Commission of Government of India headquartered in Bangalore. NAAC was established in 1994 in response to recommendations of National Policy in Education (1986). This policy was to "address the issues of deterioration in quality of education", and the Programme of Action (POA-1992) laid out strategic plans for the policies including the establishment of an independent national accreditation body. Consequently, the NAAC was established in 1994 with its headquarters at Bengaluru.

Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC)

The Internal Quality Assurance Cell in CHRIST (Deemed to be University) was established on 03 November 2003. The prime task of the IQAC is to develop a system for conscious, consistent and catalytic improvement in the overall performance of CHRIST. For this, during the post-accreditation period, it channels all efforts and measures of the Institution towards promoting its holistic academic excellence.

Centre for Education Beyond Curriculum (CEDBEC)

The Centre has been conceived as an attempt to stimulate and rediscover through experience, the perspectives in education so that individuals and institutions reflect and rediscover themselves and move towards a sustainable progress in the educational arena.
